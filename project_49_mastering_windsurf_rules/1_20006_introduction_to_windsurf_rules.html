Introduction

Welcome to Mastering Windsurf Rules course! If you've ever wished your AI pair programmer knew your project's specific coding style, or remembered your personal guidelines from one session to the next, you're in the right place. This tutorial will teach you how to transform the powerful Cascade Agent into a personalized assistant tailored perfectly to your codebase and workflows.

The Problem: Generic AI vs. Specific Projects

AI coding assistants are incredibly powerful, but out of the box, they lack project-specific context. They don't know you prefer Tabs over Spaces, use Python over JavaScript, or follow a strict naming conventions. Constantly reminding the AI of these basics is repetitive and inefficient. Windsurf Rules are designed to solve this problem.

Rules are persistent, user-defined instructions that guide the Cascade Agent. By setting up rules, you provide a permanent source of truth for your project's standards, ensuring that every code generation, edit, and suggestion is consistent and correct. This system effectively turns Cascade into an expert on your project.

Rules and Memories: Two Ways to Persist Context

Windsurf provides two powerful mechanisms for sharing and persisting context with the AI:

  • Rules: These are manually defined by you. You explicitly tell Cascade what to do and how to behave. Rules can be set globally for all your projects or locally for a specific workspace. This tutorial will focus primarily on creating and managing Rules.

  • Memories: These can be automatically generated by Cascade. As you work, the AI can identify useful context or patterns and save it as a memory for future conversations. You can also ask the AI to create a memory manually. While powerful, they are more automated than the explicit instructions provided by rules.

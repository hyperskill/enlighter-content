Introduction

You all probably heard about the "MCP" (Model Context Protocol) many times by now. And for a good reason, because they allow to give your AI intern a new tools or knowledge to overcome its limitations.

The Challenge: The AI Limiatations and default skillset

By default, your AI intern works in a vacuum. It only knows what was in its training data and what's in your current conversation. This creates several major problems:

  • Outdated Knowledge: The AI's training data has a "knowledge cutoff" date. It doesn't know about new library versions or API changes, leading it to write deprecated or broken code.
  • No Project-Wide Awareness: The AI can't see your entire private GitHub repository. It only knows about the few files you've explicitly shown it, so it can't understand how all the parts of your project fit together.
  • Inability to "See" Designs: You can't ask the AI to build a component from a Figma design, because it has no eyes. You're forced to describe the design in words, which is slow and inaccurate.

When faced with these gaps, the AI is forced to guess or "hallucinate," inventing solutions that are plausible but wrong.

The Solution: Equip Your Intern with MCPs
